Caramel Flan

History

This sweet desert has an intresting history and luckily the recipe still lived. Flan was originally created in Ancient Rome, but as the Roman empire downfall happened, the spaniards adopted this recipe known as Flan. Then spain expanded into the New world and popularized in South Mexico. In English, "Flan" means a dessert of sweetened egg custard with a caramel topping. The common misconception today is that Flan is a mexican dish, while it may have popularized largely in Mexico, the dish itself has european orgins.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Condensed Milk
  • Evaporated Milk
  • sugar
  • Vanilla extract
  • Boiling water

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees
  2. Brown sugar in a saucepan till a medium dark golden brown, but not burnt and then quickly transfer to the bottom of a pie plate
  3. In a large bowl beat the eggs and then add the 2 cans of milk and vanilla extract, mixing well
  4. Pour mixture into pie plate
  5. Using a larger pan that the pie plate will fit in, fill half way with the boiling water, place in oven, and then place pie plate in the middle of the large pan
  6. Bake for approximately 45 - 55 minutes. The center should be soft and firm but not liquidly.
  7. Let cool and then after running a knife around the edge to seperate, flip over onto a serving dish
